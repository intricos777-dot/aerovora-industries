# Aerovora Industries — Business Plan
## Bee Drone Manufacturer for Precision Plant Caretaking

---

### Executive Summary

Aerovora Industries designs, manufactures, and operates autonomous bee-inspired drones for precision plant caretaking. Our drones perform pollination, plant health monitoring, micro-spraying, and data collection for commercial agriculture. The company is fully operated by AI and robotic agents with human oversight on strategic decisions.

**Mission:** Deploy autonomous aerial swarms that replace chemical-dependent agriculture with precision, per-plant caretaking.

**Revenue Model:**
- Drone sales (hardware): $12,000–$45,000 per unit
- Drone-as-a-Service (DaaS): $800–$2,500/month per drone (includes monitoring, maintenance, data)
- Data analytics subscription: $500–$3,000/month per farm
- Pollination credits: $50–$200/acre (partnerships with orchards)

**Target Markets:**
- Greenhouse operations (indoor, controlled environment)
- Vertical farms (year-round, high-value crops)
- Orchards (almonds, apples, cherries — pollination-critical)
- Large-scale row crops (targeted monitoring and micro-spraying)

---

### Market Opportunity

- Global agricultural drone market: $18.2B by 2030 (CAGR 31.2%)
- Pollinator decline: 40% of bee colonies lost annually in US — critical need for mechanical alternatives
- Precision agriculture spend: projected $24B by 2028

| Factor | Aerovora | Competitors |
